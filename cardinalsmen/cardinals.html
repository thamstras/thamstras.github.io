Here you can find my java implimentation of The Three Muskateers game from Marc LeBlanc's GDC workshops (and my Games Programming lectures...)

For those who haven't heard of it, the rules are simple.

  • The board is 5x5
  • Muskateers start in the top right, center, and bottom left squares.
  • All other squares start with one of the Cardinal's men in them.
  • You must make a move if you can.
  • The Musketeer player starts.
  • Players take turns to move one piece.
  • Musketeers can move to any non-diagonal adjacent square which is occupied by an opponent piece. That piece is removed.
  • Cardinal's men can move to any non-diagonal adjacent square.
  • The Cardinal's men win if the three musketeers are captured: that is, they are aligned on the same row or column.
  • The musketeers win if they cannot move due to there being no enemy pieces adjacent to any musketeer. (and they are not all on the same row or column, of course)

NOTE: The program doesn't check if anyone has actually won. That's comming in a future update...
